## The Pattern

<P>
  Arabic is written <Strong>right to left</Strong>. The alphabet has <Strong>28 letters</Strong>. Each letter has a name (like <ArabicBase>بَاء</ArabicBase> for <ArabicBase>ب</ArabicBase>) and an isolated shape you can read on its own before it connects to other letters in a word.
</P>

<P>
  You already know some Arabic from reading practice. This lesson locks in the full set so later lessons on vowel marks and connected forms feel easy.
</P>

<P>
  Three letters share the same skeleton and differ only by dots: <ArabicBase>ب</ArabicBase>, <ArabicBase>ت</ArabicBase>, and <ArabicBase>ث</ArabicBase> (one, two, and three dots). Watch dot <Strong>number</Strong> and dot <Strong>position</Strong> whenever letters look similar.
</P>

<Callout title="Pronunciation focus" type="info">
  Emphatic letters like <ArabicBase>ص</ArabicBase>, <ArabicBase>ض</ArabicBase>, <ArabicBase>ط</ArabicBase>, and <ArabicBase>ظ</ArabicBase> need deeper mouth tension. You will practice them in the pronunciation track. For now, focus on recognizing their shapes.
</Callout>

## Reading Practice

<P>This lesson builds <Strong>reading confidence</Strong> step by step. Read each step aloud.</P>

<Steps>
  <Step>
    <H3>Step 1: Read a row left to right in Arabic order</H3>
    <P>Arabic rows run from right to left. Start at the rightmost letter and move left:</P>
    <ArabicLg>ا ب ت</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>alif, ba, ta</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 2: Spot the dot pattern</H3>
    <P>Look at these three letters together. How many dots does each have?</P>
    <ArabicLg>ب ت ث</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>one dot, two dots, three dots</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 3: Match letter to word start</H3>
    <P>The first letter of <ArabicBase>بَيْت</ArabicBase> is <ArabicBase>ب</ArabicBase>. The first letter of <ArabicBase>تِين</ArabicBase> is <ArabicBase>ت</ArabicBase>.</P>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 4: Read a short letter chain</H3>
    <P>Say each letter name without stopping between them:</P>
    <ArabicLg>د ر س</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>dal, ra, sin</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 5: Find the odd letter out</H3>
    <P>In <ArabicBase>س ش ص</ArabicBase>, which letter has no dots? That is <ArabicBase>ص</ArabicBase>, the emphatic sibling of <ArabicBase>س</ArabicBase>.</P>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 6: End with the last three letters</H3>
    <P>Finish the alphabet aloud:</P>
    <ArabicLg>ه و ي</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>ha, waw, ya</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<MistakeCards>
  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Ignoring dots on similar shapes</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> reading <ArabicBase>ب</ArabicBase> and <ArabicBase>ت</ArabicBase> as the same letter because the base shape matches.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> count dots every time. <ArabicBase>ب</ArabicBase> has one dot below; <ArabicBase>ت</ArabicBase> has two dots above.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Reading Arabic in English direction</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> starting at the left of <ArabicMd>ا ب ت</ArabicMd> and reading "ta, ba, alif."<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> start at the right: alif, then ba, then ta.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Confusing <ArabicBase>ح</ArabicBase> and <ArabicBase>ه</ArabicBase></CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> treating <ArabicBase>ح</ArabicBase> (deep throat h) and <ArabicBase>ه</ArabicBase> (light h) as identical because both look like hooks.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> <ArabicBase>ح</ArabicBase> has no dot and sounds deeper; <ArabicBase>ه</ArabicBase> is the ordinary h at the end of the alphabet.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>
</MistakeCards>
