## The Pattern

<P>
  Arabic letters alone do not show their vowel. Small marks called <ArabicBase>حَرَكَات</ArabicBase> (movements) sit above or below the letter to tell you which vowel to say. Together these marks are called <ArabicBase>تَشْكِيل</ArabicBase>. Learning materials for beginners almost always show full tashkeel so you can read aloud with confidence.
</P>

<P>
  Read the mark on each letter before you move to the next one. In <ArabicMd>بَيْت</ArabicMd>, the first letter has fatha (<Transliteration>ba</Transliteration>), the middle letter has sukun (<Transliteration>y</Transliteration> with no full vowel), and the last letter has fatha again (<Transliteration>t</Transliteration>).
</P>

<Callout title="Pronunciation focus" type="info">
  Fatha, kasra, and damma are <Strong>short</Strong> vowels. Long vowels use letter carriers like <ArabicBase>ا</ArabicBase>, <ArabicBase>و</ArabicBase>, and <ArabicBase>ي</ArabicBase>. You will practice vowel length in the pronunciation track. For now, keep short vowels light and quick.
</Callout>

## Build Sentences

<P>Build readable syllables step by step. Read each step aloud.</P>

<Steps>
  <Step>
    <H3>Step 1: Start with a bare letter</H3>
    <P>You already know the letter shape from Lesson 1:</P>
    <ArabicLg>ب</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Letter ba, no vowel mark yet</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 2: Add fatha for "ba"</H3>
    <P>Put a fatha above the letter:</P>
    <ArabicLg>بَ</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>ba (short a)</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 3: Swap to kasra for "bi"</H3>
    <P>Move the mark below the letter:</P>
    <ArabicLg>بِ</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>bi (short i)</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 4: Swap to damma for "bu"</H3>
    <P>Put the small و shape above the letter:</P>
    <ArabicLg>بُ</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>bu (short u)</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 5: Close the syllable with sukun</H3>
    <P>Sukun means "rest." The letter stops with no vowel:</P>
    <ArabicLg>بْ</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>b (as in the start of "stop")</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 6: Read a full voweled word</H3>
    <P>Combine marks across letters in <ArabicBase>بَيْت</ArabicBase>:</P>
    <ArabicLg>بَيْت</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>bayt (house)</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<MistakeCards>
  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Mixing up fatha and damma</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> reading both <ArabicBase>بَ</ArabicBase> and <ArabicBase>بُ</ArabicBase> as the same sound because the mark sits above the letter.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> fatha is a short slash (a sound). Damma looks like a small <ArabicBase>و</ArabicBase> (u sound).
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Skipping sukun</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> adding a vowel where sukun sits, reading <ArabicMd>بِنْت</ArabicMd> as three open syllables with extra vowels.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> stop cleanly on <ArabicBase>نْ</ArabicBase> before the <ArabicBase>ت</ArabicBase>. The n rests, then t starts the last syllable.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Ignoring shadda</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> reading <ArabicBase>مَدَّ</ArabicBase> with a single light <Transliteration>d</Transliteration>.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> shadda doubles the letter. Hold the consonant slightly: <Transliteration>mad-da</Transliteration>, with a stronger middle <Transliteration>d</Transliteration>.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>
</MistakeCards>
