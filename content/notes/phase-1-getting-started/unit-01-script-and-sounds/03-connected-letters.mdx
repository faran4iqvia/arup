## The Pattern

<P>
  Arabic is written like cursive handwriting: most letters <Strong>connect</Strong> to the next letter in the same word. A letter changes its shape depending on where it sits: alone, at the start, in the middle, or at the end.
</P>

<P>
  There are up to <Strong>four shapes</Strong> per letter. You already know the <Strong>isolated</Strong> shape from Lesson 1. Inside words you will see:
</P>

<UL>
  <LI><Strong>Initial</Strong> (<ArabicBase>شَكْل أَوَّل</ArabicBase>): first letter of a word, connects to the left</LI>
  <LI><Strong>Medial</Strong> (<ArabicBase>شَكْل وَسَط</ArabicBase>): middle of a word, connects on both sides</LI>
  <LI><Strong>Final</Strong> (<ArabicBase>شَكْل آخِر</ArabicBase>): last letter of a word, connects from the right only</LI>
</UL>

<P>
  Read words from <Strong>right to left</Strong>. Follow the ink line from one letter to the next.
</P>

### Six letters that do not connect forward

<P>
  These letters connect <Strong>from the right</Strong> but never join to the letter after them on the left. They always stand slightly apart from what follows:
</P>

<ArabicLg>ا  د  ذ  ر  ز  و</ArabicLg>

<P>
  Letters: alif, dal, dhal, ra, zay, waw. When you see <ArabicBase>ر</ArabicBase> or <ArabicBase>و</ArabicBase> inside a word, expect a small break after them before the next letter.
</P>

<BilingualBlock arabic="كَبِير" transliteration="kabīr" english="Big: ba sits in the middle between kaf and ya" />

<BilingualBlock arabic="كَتَبَ" transliteration="kataba" english="He wrote: ka + ta + ba (connected chain)" />

<BilingualBlock arabic="مَدْرَسَة" transliteration="madrasa" english="School: watch medial dal and final ta with ta marbuta" />

<BilingualBlock arabic="سَلَام" transliteration="salām" english="Peace: sin connects, lam connects, alif stands at the end" />

<Callout title="Reading tip" type="info">
  Do not panic if a middle letter looks unfamiliar. Trace the dots first, then the base shape. The same letter from Lesson 1 is hiding inside the connection.
</Callout>

## Build Sentences

<P>Train your eye to follow connections step by step. Read each step aloud.</P>

<Steps>
  <Step>
    <H3>Step 1: Find the first letter</H3>
    <P>In <ArabicMd>كِتَاب</ArabicMd>, start at the far right. The first letter is <ArabicBase>ك</ArabicBase> with kasra (<Transliteration>ki</Transliteration>).</P>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 2: Follow the connection to the middle</H3>
    <P>Move left to the medial <ArabicBase>ت</ArabicBase> with fatha (<Transliteration>ta</Transliteration>). The line links <ArabicBase>ك</ArabicBase> to <ArabicBase>ت</ArabicBase>.</P>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 3: Reach the final letter</H3>
    <P>End on final <ArabicBase>ب</ArabicBase> at the left edge of the word: <Transliteration>āb</Transliteration>. The alif before it carries the long vowel. Full word: <Transliteration>kitāb</Transliteration>.</P>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 4: Read a verb shape chain</H3>
    <P>In <ArabicMd>كَتَبَ</ArabicMd>, all three letters connect. Final <ArabicBase>ب</ArabicBase> takes fatha and ends the word.</P>
    <ArabicLg>كَتَبَ</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>He wrote</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 5: Spot a non connecting letter</H3>
    <P>In <ArabicMd>مَدْرَسَة</ArabicMd>, the <ArabicBase>ر</ArabicBase> does not join to the <ArabicBase>س</ArabicBase> after it. Pause slightly at <ArabicBase>ر</ArabicBase>, then read <ArabicBase>سَة</ArabicBase>.</P>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 6: Read a full connected word</H3>
    <P>Put it together with vowels from Lesson 2:</P>
    <ArabicLg>مَدْرَسَة</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>School</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<MistakeCards>
  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Treating every letter as isolated</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> looking for the Lesson 1 isolated shape inside <ArabicMd>كِتَاب</ArabicMd> and thinking it is a new letter.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> the medial <ArabicBase>ت</ArabicBase> and final <ArabicBase>ب</ArabicBase> are the same letters with joined shapes. Follow the connection line.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Expecting every letter to join forward</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> assuming <ArabicBase>ر</ArabicBase> in <ArabicMd>مَدْرَسَة</ArabicMd> will connect to <ArabicBase>س</ArabicBase> the way <ArabicBase>د</ArabicBase> connects to <ArabicBase>ر</ArabicBase>.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> <ArabicBase>ر</ArabicBase> is one of the six non connecting letters. Leave a tiny break before <ArabicBase>س</ArabicBase>.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Reading left to right inside the word</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> starting at the left of <ArabicMd>سَلَام</ArabicMd> and reading <Transliteration>mālas</Transliteration>.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> start at the right with <ArabicBase>س</ArabicBase>, then <ArabicBase>ل</ArabicBase>, then <ArabicBase>ا</ArabicBase>, then <ArabicBase>م</ArabicBase>: <Transliteration>salām</Transliteration>.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>
</MistakeCards>
