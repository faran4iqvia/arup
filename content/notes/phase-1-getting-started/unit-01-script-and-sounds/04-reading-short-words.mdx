## The Pattern

<P>
  Short Arabic words are the best place to practice everything from Lessons 1 to 3: letter shapes, vowel marks, and smooth connections. Use a simple reading rhythm:
</P>

<OL>
  <LI>Start at the <Strong>far right</Strong> of the word.</LI>
  <LI>Read each letter with its vowel mark in order, moving left.</LI>
  <LI>Keep the word in one breath unless sukun tells you to stop briefly on a consonant.</LI>
  <LI>Do not translate letter by letter into English order. Let the Arabic flow right to left.</LI>
</OL>

<P>
  <Strong>Two letter words</Strong> like <ArabicBase>أَب</ArabicBase> and <ArabicBase>يَد</ArabicBase> are often one open syllable plus a closed ending. <Strong>Three letter words</Strong> like <ArabicBase>قَمَر</ArabicBase> and <ArabicBase>وَلَد</ArabicBase> usually give you three clear beats: <Transliteration>qa ma r</Transliteration>, <Transliteration>wa la d</Transliteration>.
</P>

<Callout title="Reading tip" type="info">
  You do not need to know every meaning yet. The goal is to move your eyes across the word once and say it smoothly. Meaning sticks faster after the mouth learns the shape.
</Callout>

## Build Sentences

<P>Build reading speed step by step. Read each step aloud without pausing between letters unless sukun requires it.</P>

<Steps>
  <Step>
    <H3>Step 1: Two letter family words</H3>
    <P>Start with the shortest words on the right edge:</P>
    <ArabicLg>أَب</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Father</TranslationBlock>
    <ArabicLg>أُم</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Mother</TranslationBlock>
    <ArabicLg>أَخ</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Brother</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 2: Two letter body words</H3>
    <ArabicLg>يَد</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Hand</TranslationBlock>
    <ArabicLg>فَم</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Mouth</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 3: Three letter words with sukun</H3>
    <P>Hold the middle consonant lightly where sukun appears:</P>
    <ArabicLg>شَمْس</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Sun</TranslationBlock>
    <ArabicLg>وَلَد</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Child</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 4: Three letter words with long vowels</H3>
    <ArabicLg>نَار</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Fire</TranslationBlock>
    <ArabicLg>قَمَر</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Moon</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 5: Read a word you already know plus a new one</H3>
    <P>Combine review and new reading in one breath:</P>
    <ArabicMd>كِتَاب · مَاء</ArabicMd>
    <TranslationBlock>Book · water</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 6: Read a row of short words</H3>
    <P>Read right to left across the row:</P>
    <ArabicLg>أَب · وَلَد · قَمَر</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Father · child · moon</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<MistakeCards>
  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Adding extra vowels before sukun</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> reading <ArabicMd>شَمْس</ArabicMd> as <Transliteration>sha mes</Transliteration> with two open syllables.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> sukun rests on the mim: <Transliteration>shams</Transliteration>, one smooth word.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Reading the word from left to right</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> starting at the left of <ArabicMd>وَلَد</ArabicMd> and saying <Transliteration>dal la wa</Transliteration>.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> start at the right with <ArabicBase>و</ArabicBase>, then <ArabicBase>ل</ArabicBase>, then <ArabicBase>د</ArabicBase>: <Transliteration>walad</Transliteration>.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Pausing between every letter</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> chopping <ArabicMd>قَمَر</ArabicMd> into isolated letter names.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> keep the connections flowing: <Transliteration>qa mar</Transliteration>, two light beats, not three separate stops.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>
</MistakeCards>
