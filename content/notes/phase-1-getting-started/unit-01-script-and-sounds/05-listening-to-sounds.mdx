## The Pattern

<P>
  Arabic has several sounds that do not exist in English, and pairs of sounds that look almost identical on the page. The best way to train your ear is a <Strong>minimal pair</Strong>: two words that differ by only one sound. Say both aloud. Feel where the sound lives in your mouth and throat.
</P>

<P>
  You do not need audio files to start. Read each pair slowly, exaggerate the difference, then read it again at normal speed. Your mouth teaches your ear.
</P>

## Build Sentences

<P>Train your ear in six short steps. Say each pair twice: slowly, then naturally.</P>

<Steps>
  <Step>
    <H3>Step 1: Dot trio on the same shape</H3>
    <P>Listen to b, t, and th in isolation:</P>
    <ArabicLg>بَ · تَ · ثَ</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>ba · ta · tha</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 2: Throat h vs light h</H3>
    <ArabicLg>حَل · هَل</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Solution · question marker (hear the h change)</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 3: Throat h vs kh</H3>
    <ArabicLg>حَل · خَل</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Solution · (kh sound on the second word; not a common word alone, but good for ear training)</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 4: k vs q in real words</H3>
    <ArabicLg>كَلْب · قَلْب</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Dog · heart</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 5: ayn vs hamza</H3>
    <ArabicLg>عَيْن · أَيْن</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Eye · where?</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 6: Plain s vs emphatic ṣ</H3>
    <ArabicLg>سَرِير · صَرِير</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Bed · creaking sound</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<MistakeCards>
  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Replacing q with English k</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> reading <ArabicMd>قَلْب</ArabicMd> exactly like <ArabicMd>كَلْب</ArabicMd>.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> push <ArabicBase>ق</ArabicBase> deeper in the throat. The words should sound clearly different.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Skipping emphatic letters</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> reading <ArabicMd>صَرِير</ArabicMd> with a plain English s.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> tighten the mouth slightly for <ArabicBase>ص</ArabicBase>. Compare again with <ArabicMd>سَرِير</ArabicMd> until you feel the contrast.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Treating ayn like a silent letter</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> reading <ArabicMd>عَيْن</ArabicMd> like <ArabicMd>أَيْن</ArabicMd> without the deep throat sound.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> <ArabicBase>ع</ArabicBase> has a strong throat presence. Hold it briefly, then finish with <Transliteration>ayn</Transliteration>.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>
</MistakeCards>
