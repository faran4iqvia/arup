## The Pattern

<P>
  Arabic greetings work in <Strong>pairs</Strong>. One person opens with a set phrase. The other person answers with the matching reply. You do not need a verb like "I am" for these phrases. Learn them as fixed expressions, the way English speakers say "How do you do?" as a whole chunk.
</P>

<P>
  The most common formal hello is <ArabicBase>السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُم</ArabicBase>. The reply mirrors the greeting: <ArabicBase>وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّلَام</ArabicBase>. Notice how the reply starts with <ArabicBase>وَ</ArabicBase> meaning "and."
</P>

<BilingualBlock arabic="السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُم" transliteration="as salāmu ʿalaykum" english="Peace be upon you" />

<BilingualBlock arabic="وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّلَام" transliteration="wa ʿalaykumu s salām" english="And upon you peace" />

<BilingualBlock arabic="صَبَاحُ الخَيْر" transliteration="ṣabāḥu l khayr" english="Good morning" />

<BilingualBlock arabic="صَبَاحُ النُّور" transliteration="ṣabāḥ an nūr" english="Good morning (reply, literally: morning of light)" />

<Callout title="Pronunciation focus" type="info">
  In <ArabicBase>السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُم</ArabicBase>, stress the second syllable of <Transliteration>salām</Transliteration> and keep the ending <Transliteration>kum</Transliteration> short and clear. See the pronunciation track lesson on connected speech when you want more detail.
</Callout>

## Build Sentences

<P>Practice greeting exchanges step by step. Read each line aloud.</P>

<Steps>
  <Step>
    <H3>Step 1: Open with peace</H3>
    <P>Say the formal hello:</P>
    <ArabicLg>السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُم</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Peace be upon you</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 2: Answer the greeting</H3>
    <P>Your partner says <ArabicBase>السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُم</ArabicBase>. You reply:</P>
    <ArabicLg>وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّلَام</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>And upon you peace</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 3: Add a friendly welcome</H3>
    <P>After the exchange, you can add a warm phrase:</P>
    <ArabicLg>أَهْلًا وَسَهْلًا</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>Welcome (literally: family and ease)</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 4: Morning greeting pair</H3>
    <P>In the morning, swap the peace greeting for:</P>
    <ArabicMd>صَبَاحُ الخَيْر</ArabicMd>
    <TranslationBlock>Good morning</TranslationBlock>
    <P>Reply with:</P>
    <ArabicMd>صَبَاحُ النُّور</ArabicMd>
    <TranslationBlock>Good morning (reply)</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 5: Close the conversation</H3>
    <P>When leaving, choose one goodbye:</P>
    <ArabicMd>مَعَ السَّلَامَة</ArabicMd>
    <TranslationBlock>Goodbye</TranslationBlock>
    <P>Or:</P>
    <ArabicMd>إِلَى اللِّقَاء</ArabicMd>
    <TranslationBlock>Until we meet again</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<MistakeCards>
  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Reply without <ArabicBase>وَ</ArabicBase></CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> repeating <ArabicBase>السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُم</ArabicBase> back exactly.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> <ArabicBase>وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّلَام</ArabicBase>. The reply adds <ArabicBase>وَ</ArabicBase> at the start.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Mixing morning and evening phrases</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> saying <ArabicBase>مَسَاءُ الخَيْر</ArabicBase> at 8 a.m.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> use <ArabicBase>صَبَاحُ الخَيْر</ArabicBase> before noon, and <ArabicBase>مَسَاءُ الخَيْر</ArabicBase> in the afternoon and evening.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Translating word by word in English order</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> reading <ArabicBase>مَعَ السَّلَامَة</ArabicBase> as "with the safety" and stopping there.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> treat it as one goodbye formula meaning "go in peace," like "goodbye" in English.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>
</MistakeCards>
