## The Pattern

<P>
  Arabic often builds simple sentences <Strong>without a verb</Strong>. English says "This is a book." Arabic says <ArabicMd>هَذَا كِتَاب</ArabicMd>, literally "this book." The word "is" is understood. This is called a <Strong>nominal sentence</Strong>: it starts with a noun or demonstrative, not a verb.
</P>

<P>
  Use <ArabicBase>هَذَا</ArabicBase> before <Strong>masculine</Strong> nouns and <ArabicBase>هَذِهِ</ArabicBase> before <Strong>feminine</Strong> nouns. Feminine nouns often end in <ArabicBase>ة</ArabicBase> (ta marbuta), like <ArabicBase>مَدْرَسَة</ArabicBase>.
</P>

<P>
  Pattern: <ArabicBase>هَذَا</ArabicBase> + masculine noun  |  <ArabicBase>هَذِهِ</ArabicBase> + feminine noun
</P>

## Build Sentences

<P>Build "this is a…" sentences step by step. Read each result aloud.</P>

<Steps>
  <Step>
    <H3>Step 1: Pick the demonstrative</H3>

    <P>
      Masculine noun like <ArabicBase>كِتَاب</ArabicBase>? Start with <ArabicBase>هَذَا</ArabicBase>. Feminine noun like <ArabicBase>مَدْرَسَة</ArabicBase>? Start with <ArabicBase>هَذِهِ</ArabicBase>.
    </P>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 2: Add one noun</H3>
    <P>Combine demonstrative + noun. No extra word for "is":</P>
    <ArabicLg>هَذَا كِتَاب</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>This is a book</TranslationBlock>
    <ArabicLg>هَذِهِ مَدْرَسَة</ArabicLg>
    <TranslationBlock>This is a school</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 3: Swap the noun</H3>
    <P>Keep <ArabicBase>هَذَا</ArabicBase>. Change the noun only:</P>
    <ArabicMd>هَذَا قَلَم</ArabicMd>
    <TranslationBlock>This is a pen</TranslationBlock>
    <ArabicMd>هَذَا بَاب</ArabicMd>
    <TranslationBlock>This is a door</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 4: Switch to feminine</H3>
    <P>Use <ArabicBase>هَذِهِ</ArabicBase> with feminine nouns:</P>
    <ArabicMd>هَذِهِ غُرْفَة</ArabicMd>
    <TranslationBlock>This is a room</TranslationBlock>
    <ArabicMd>هَذِهِ طَاوِلَة</ArabicMd>
    <TranslationBlock>This is a table</TranslationBlock>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    <H3>Step 5: Point and name in one breath</H3>

    <P>
      In conversation, point at an object and say the full nominal sentence. Mixed English explanation with Arabic inline should stay readable:
    </P>

    <P>
      Point at a pen and say <ArabicMd>هَذَا قَلَم</ArabicMd>. Point at a car and say <ArabicMd>هَذِهِ سَيَّارَة</ArabicMd>. The sentence flows right to left in Arabic while this explanation stays left to right in English.
    </P>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<MistakeCards>
  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Using <ArabicBase>هَذَا</ArabicBase> with every noun</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> <ArabicMd>هَذَا مَدْرَسَة</ArabicMd> (masculine demonstrative + feminine noun).<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> <ArabicMd>هَذِهِ مَدْرَسَة</ArabicMd>. Match <ArabicBase>هَذِهِ</ArabicBase> to feminine nouns.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Inserting English "is" in Arabic</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> trying to add a word for "is" between <ArabicBase>هَذَا</ArabicBase> and <ArabicBase>كِتَاب</ArabicBase>.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> <ArabicMd>هَذَا كِتَاب</ArabicMd> alone. Two words are enough.
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>

  <ShadCnCard>
    <CardHeader>
      <CardTitle>Reading Arabic in English word order</CardTitle>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <P>
        <Strong>Wrong:</Strong> saying "book this" because English puts the noun last.<br />
        <Strong>Right:</Strong> read <ArabicMd>هَذَا كِتَاب</ArabicMd> right to left as a unit: "this (is a) book."
      </P>
    </CardContent>
  </ShadCnCard>
</MistakeCards>
